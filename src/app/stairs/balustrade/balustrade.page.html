 Suitable handrails for common stairs in blocks of flats With the entrance storey of a dwelling: if a flight comprises three or more risers, provide a suitable continuous handrail:
  • on each side of the flights, and
  • on each side of any intermediate landings.
In buildings other than dwellings and in buildings that contain flats and do not have passenger lifts: on the common stairs, if they comprise two or more risers, provide suitable continuous handrails. Landing Balustrade(HL) height is 1000mm and stair balustrade(HS) is 900mm:
  • on each side of the flights, and
  • on each side of the landings
In situations other than those described above:
  • If the stairs are less than 1000mm wide: provide a handrail on one or both sides.
  • If the stairs are 1000mm wider: provide handrail on both sides.
In public buildings and any building where stairs are intended for use by people with disabilities: provide handrails beside the two bottom steps.
In all buildings, design handrails as follows:
  • Position the top of the handrail(HL and HS) 900-1000mm from the pitch line or floor.
  • The handrail may form the top of a guarding if you can match the heights,
 In buildings other than dwellings
  • Position the top of the handrail(HL) 900-1100mm from the landing floor.
  • Position the top of the handrail(HL) 900-1000mm from the stair pitch line.
  • Where there is full-height structural guarding, if you provide a second(lower) handrail, ensure that the vertical height from the pitch line of the steps or the surface of the ramp to the top of the second(lower) handrail is 600mm.
  • Use continuous handrail along the flights and landings of a ramped or stepped flight.
  • Ensure that handrails do not project into an access route.
  • Ensure that handrail contasts vissuall with the background against which it is seen, without being highly reflective.
  • Use a surface for the handrail that is slip-resistant and not cold to touch.
  • Finish the end of the handrail in a way that reduces the risk of clothing being caught.
